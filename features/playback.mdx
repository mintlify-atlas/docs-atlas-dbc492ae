ThystTV offers comprehensive playback features for live streams, VODs, and clips, giving you full control over your viewing experience.

## Quality Selection

Choose the video quality that best matches your network conditions and preferences.

### Available Qualities

ThystTV supports all Twitch quality options:

- **Source**: Original broadcast quality (highest bitrate)
- **1080p60**: 1080p resolution at 60 FPS  
- **1080p**: 1080p resolution at 30 FPS
- **720p60**: 720p resolution at 60 FPS
- **720p**: 720p resolution at 30 FPS  
- **480p**: Standard definition
- **360p**: Lower quality for slower connections
- **160p**: Minimum quality
- **Audio Only**: No video, audio stream only

<Note>
Available qualities depend on what the streamer is broadcasting. Not all streams will have every quality option.
</Note>

### Changing Quality

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open player controls">
    Tap the video once to show player controls.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Tap quality button">
    Tap the **Quality** button (gear icon or quality label).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select quality">
    Choose your preferred quality from the list. The current quality is highlighted.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The video will briefly pause and resume at the new quality.

### Auto Quality Selection

ThystTV can automatically select the best quality based on your settings:

**Default Quality Priority**:
1. Checks your **preferred quality** setting (if configured)
2. Falls back to **Source** if preferred quality unavailable  
3. Selects highest available quality if Source unavailable

Configure default quality in **Settings** > **Player** > **Default Quality**.

### Quality Indicators

The current quality is displayed:
- On the quality button in player controls
- In the player settings menu
- During quality transitions

<Tip>
For streams with fast action or detailed graphics, higher framerates (60 FPS) provide smoother playback. For slower content, 30 FPS qualities use less bandwidth.
</Tip>

## Playback Speed

Adjust video playback speed for VODs and clips (not available for live streams).

### Available Speeds

ThystTV supports a wide range of playback speeds:

- **0.25x** to **4.0x**: Fine-grained control
- **Common presets**: 0.5x, 0.75x, 1.0x (normal), 1.25x, 1.5x, 2.0x
- **Custom speeds**: Use the slider for any speed in the range

### Changing Speed

#### Method 1: Speed Dialog

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open player controls">
    Tap the video once to show controls.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Tap speed button">
    Tap the **Speed** button (shows current speed, e.g., "1.0x").
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Adjust speed">
    - Tap a **speed chip** for preset speeds, or
    - Use the **slider** for precise control
    - Tap **-0.05** or **+0.05** buttons for fine adjustments
  </Step>
</Steps>

#### Method 2: Gesture Control

Swipe horizontally in the bottom zone of the screen (see [Gesture Controls](/features/gesture-controls) for details).

### Speed Display

- Speed is shown on the speed button (e.g., "1.50x")  
- "1.0x" may include "(Normal)" label
- Changes apply immediately without buffering

### Use Cases

<Accordion title="Slow Motion (0.25x - 0.75x)">
  Perfect for:
  - Analyzing gameplay frame-by-frame
  - Catching details in fast-paced content  
  - Learning techniques from skilled players
  - Enjoying cinematic moments
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Normal Speed (1.0x)">
  The default playback speed, matching the original content timing.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Fast Playback (1.25x - 2.0x)">
  Ideal for:
  - Watching VODs of long streams
  - Speeding through slower segments  
  - Consuming more content in less time
  - Reviewing streams you've already seen
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Very Fast (2.0x - 4.0x)">
  Useful for:
  - Quick VOD previews
  - Finding specific moments in long videos
  - Power users comfortable with rapid playback
</Accordion>

<Note>
Playback speed is only available for VODs and clips. Live streams always play at 1.0x.
</Note>

## Audio Only Mode

Listen to streams without video, saving bandwidth and battery.

### Enabling Audio Only

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open player controls">
    Tap the video once to show controls.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Audio Only">
    Either:
    - Tap the **Audio Only** button (speaker icon), or
    - Open **Quality** menu and select **Audio Only**
  </Step>
</Steps>

The video area shows an audio-only indicator while the stream continues playing.

### Benefits

- **Reduced bandwidth**: Uses ~128 kbps instead of several Mbps
- **Battery savings**: No video decoding or screen updates  
- **Background listening**: Great for music streams or podcasts
- **Mobile data friendly**: Significantly lower data usage

### Returning to Video

Tap the **Audio Only** button again or select any video quality from the quality menu.

<Tip>
Audio Only mode is perfect for:
- Music & DJ streams
- Podcasts and talk shows  
- Keeping a stream running while multitasking
- Conserving mobile data
</Tip>

## Volume Control

### Methods

1. **Hardware buttons**: Use device volume buttons (standard Android behavior)
2. **Volume dialog**: Tap volume button in player controls for fine control  
3. **Gesture control**: Swipe up/down on right half of screen (landscape mode)

### Volume Dialog

The volume dialog provides:
- **Slider**: Precise volume adjustment (0-100%)
- **Mute button**: Instant mute/unmute toggle  
- **Current level**: Percentage display

### Audio Compressor (Android 9+)

The audio compressor reduces volume differences for more consistent listening:

- **Enabled**: Loud moments are quieter, quiet moments are louder  
- **Disabled**: Original dynamic range preserved

To toggle:

1. Open player controls
2. Tap the **Audio Compressor** button (waveform icon)
3. Icon changes to indicate on/off state

Configure default in **Settings** > **Player** > **Audio Compressor**.

<Note>
Audio compressor requires Android 9 (API 28) or higher. The button is hidden on older Android versions.
</Note>

## Video Resize Modes

Adjust how the video fits your screen in landscape mode.

### Available Modes

Cycle through resize modes by tapping the **Aspect Ratio** button:

1. **Fit**: Entire video visible, may have black bars (default)
2. **Fill**: Video fills screen, may crop edges  
3. **Zoom**: Zooms in slightly, moderate cropping
4. **Fixed Height**: Maintains video height, adjusts width
5. **Fixed Width**: Maintains video width, adjusts height

### When to Use Each Mode

<Accordion title="Fit (Default)">
  Best for most content. Shows the complete video frame without cropping, adding letterboxing if the video aspect ratio doesn't match your screen.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Fill">
  Eliminates black bars by filling the entire screen. Use when you don't mind losing content at the edges, such as for gameplay streams where UI elements are in the center.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Zoom">
  A compromise between Fit and Fill. Provides some cropping to reduce black bars while keeping most content visible.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Fixed Height / Fixed Width">
  Advanced modes for specific aspect ratio needs. Useful for unusual video dimensions or custom display setups.
</Accordion>

Your resize mode preference is saved per orientation (portrait vs. landscape).

## Subtitles / Closed Captions

View subtitles when available on VODs and clips.

### Enabling Subtitles

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open player menu">
    Tap the **Menu** button (three dots) in player controls.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Toggle subtitles">
    If subtitles are available, you'll see either:
    - **Show Subtitles** (when off)
    - **Hide Subtitles** (when on)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select language">
    If multiple subtitle tracks exist, choose your preferred language.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Subtitle Settings

Customize subtitle appearance in Android system settings:

1. Go to device **Settings** > **Accessibility** > **Caption preferences**
2. Adjust text size, font, colors, and background
3. Changes apply to all apps including ThystTV

<Note>
Subtitles are only available if the content creator has uploaded caption files. Most live streams do not have subtitles.
</Note>

## Advanced Playback Options

### Seek Controls

Navigate through VODs and clips:

- **Progress bar**: Drag the scrubber to jump to any point  
- **Rewind button**: Skip backward (default: 10 seconds)
- **Fast forward button**: Skip forward (default: 10 seconds)
- **Gesture seeking**: Swipe horizontally in top zone (landscape)

Configure skip duration in **Settings** > **Player** > **Rewind/Forward Duration**.

### Live Stream Features

#### Seek to Live

If you've paused or rewound a live stream:

1. Tap the **Seek Live** button (appears when not at live point)
2. Stream jumps to current live broadcast  
3. Minimizes latency

#### Restart Stream

Reload the stream connection:

1. Tap the **Restart** button in player controls
2. Stream reconnects from the current point  
3. Useful for resolving playback issues

### Picture-in-Picture (PiP)

Continue watching while using other apps:

1. While playing, press the **Home** button or switch apps  
2. Video shrinks to a floating window
3. Drag the window to reposition it
4. Tap the window to return to full player

Configure PiP behavior in **Settings** > **Player** > **Picture-in-Picture**.

<Note>
Picture-in-Picture requires Android 8.0 (Oreo) or higher and must be enabled in Android settings.
</Note>

## Sleep Timer

Automatically stop playback after a set duration.

### Setting Sleep Timer

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open sleep timer">
    Tap the **Sleep Timer** button in player controls (clock icon).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose duration">
    Select a preset time (15, 30, 60 minutes) or use the time picker for custom duration.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Timer starts counting down. Playback stops when time expires.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Canceling Sleep Timer

Open the sleep timer dialog and tap **Stop** or **Cancel**.

### Lock Screen (Optional)

Enable **Lock on Sleep** in settings to lock your device when the sleep timer expires.

<Tip>
Perfect for falling asleep to streams. Set a 30-60 minute timer so the stream stops automatically.
</Tip>

## Troubleshooting

<Accordion title="Video keeps buffering">
  Try these steps:
  1. Switch to a lower quality (720p or 480p)
  2. Check your internet connection speed
  3. Close other apps using bandwidth
  4. Use Audio Only mode if video isn't important
  5. Restart the stream
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Playback speed isn't available">
  Playback speed only works for VODs and clips, not live streams. Verify you're watching recorded content.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Quality changes automatically">
  If you have adaptive quality enabled, ThystTV may automatically adjust based on network conditions. Disable this in Settings > Player > Adaptive Quality.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Audio and video out of sync">
  1. Pause and resume playback
  2. Change quality to a different option, then back
  3. Restart the stream
  4. Check if the issue occurs with other players (may be source issue)
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="No audio in Audio Only mode">
  - Ensure device volume is not muted
  - Check that media volume (not ringtone) is turned up
  - Verify the stream has audio (some categories are silent)
  - Try switching to video mode and back
</Accordion>
