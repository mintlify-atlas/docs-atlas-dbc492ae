Gesture Controls provide intuitive swipe-based interactions for adjusting playback without obscuring the video. Control volume, brightness, seeking, and playback speed with simple touch gestures.

## Overview

ThystTV's gesture system divides the screen into zones, with each zone controlling a different aspect of playback:

- **Left half (vertical swipe)**: Brightness
- **Right half (vertical swipe)**: Volume  
- **Top zone (horizontal swipe)**: Seek forward/backward _(VODs only)_
- **Bottom zone (horizontal swipe)**: Playback speed _(VODs only)_

<Note>
Gesture controls only work in **landscape mode** when the player is **maximized** and **controls are hidden**. Tap once to hide the controls, then swipe to use gestures.
</Note>

## Enabling Gesture Controls

Gesture controls are enabled by default. To toggle:

1. Go to **Settings** > **Player** > **Gestures**
2. Toggle **Enable Gestures** on or off

## Volume Control

### How to Use

<Steps>
  <Step title="Position your finger">
    Place your finger on the **right half** of the screen.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Swipe vertically">
    - Swipe **up** to increase volume
    - Swipe **down** to decrease volume
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Check the indicator">
    A volume indicator appears showing:
    - Speaker icon (or muted icon at 0%)
    - Volume percentage (0-100%)
    - Progress bar
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Behavior

- Adjusts system media volume (same as hardware volume buttons)
- Changes apply immediately during the swipe
- Volume is clamped between 0% (mute) and 100% (max)
- Indicator auto-hides after 800ms

<Tip>
For precise volume adjustments, use slower swipe movements. The gesture system responds proportionally to your swipe distance.
</Tip>

## Brightness Control

### How to Use

<Steps>
  <Step title="Position your finger">
    Place your finger on the **left half** of the screen.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Swipe vertically">
    - Swipe **up** to increase brightness
    - Swipe **down** to decrease brightness  
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Check the indicator">
    A brightness indicator shows:
    - Sun icon
    - Brightness percentage (5-100%) or "Auto"
    - Progress bar
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Behavior

- Adjusts app-level brightness (only affects ThystTV, not system brightness)
- Minimum brightness: 5% (prevents completely black screen)
- Swipe to the very bottom to enable **Auto** mode (uses system brightness)
- Setting persists only for the current session
- Returns to your system brightness when you exit the player

<Note>
Brightness changes only apply while the video is playing. Your device's global brightness setting remains unchanged.
</Note>

## Seeking (VODs Only)

Seeking gestures allow you to skip forward or rewind through video content.

### How to Use

<Steps>
  <Step title="Position your finger">
    Place your finger in the **top portion** of the screen (configurable zone split).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Swipe horizontally">
    - Swipe **right** to seek forward  
    - Swipe **left** to rewind
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Check the indicator">
    The seek indicator displays:
    - Plus (+) or minus (-) icon
    - Current position / total duration
    - Progress bar showing position in video
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Behavior

- **Base sensitivity**: Full screen swipe = 90 seconds of seek time
- **Sensitivity multiplier**: Adjustable in settings (0.5x, 1x, 2x)
- Seeking updates in real-time as you swipe
- Position is clamped between 0:00 and video duration
- Indicator auto-hides after 800ms

<Accordion title="Why can't I seek on live streams?">
  Seeking gestures are disabled for live streams because there's no recorded content to skip through. For VODs (video on demand) and clips, seeking works as expected.
</Accordion>

## Playback Speed (VODs Only)

Adjust how fast or slow the video plays.

### How to Use  

<Steps>
  <Step title="Position your finger">
    Place your finger in the **bottom portion** of the screen (below the seek zone).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Swipe horizontally">
    - Swipe **right** to increase speed
    - Swipe **left** to decrease speed
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Check the indicator">
    The speed indicator shows:
    - Speedometer icon
    - Current speed (e.g., "1.50x")
    - Progress bar (mapped from 0.25x to 4.0x)
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Behavior

- **Speed range**: 0.25x (very slow) to 4.0x (very fast)
- **Increments**: 0.05x steps (rounds to nearest 0.05)
- **Base sensitivity**: Full swipe = 2.0x speed change  
- **Sensitivity multiplier**: Adjustable in settings
- Normal speed (1.0x) is the default
- Speed changes apply immediately

<Tip>
Playback speed is great for:
- **0.5x-0.75x**: Catching details in fast-paced gameplay
- **1.25x-1.5x**: Speeding through slower content
- **2.0x+**: Quickly reviewing VODs
</Tip>

## Gesture Settings

Customize gesture behavior to match your preferences.

### Sensitivity

Controls how much change occurs per swipe distance:

- **Low (0.5x)**: Requires larger swipes for the same change (more precise)
- **Medium (1x)**: Default balanced sensitivity _(recommended)_  
- **High (2x)**: Smaller swipes cause larger changes (faster adjustments)

To adjust:

1. Go to **Settings** > **Player** > **Gestures**  
2. Select **Sensitivity**
3. Choose Low, Medium, or High

### Zone Split

Defines where the seek zone ends and the speed zone begins (for horizontal swipes):

- **40%**: Larger speed zone, smaller seek zone
- **50%**: Equal split _(default)_
- **60%**: Larger seek zone, smaller speed zone  

To adjust:

1. Go to **Settings** > **Player** > **Gestures**
2. Select **Zone Split**  
3. Choose your preferred ratio

<Note>
If you primarily use seeking, choose 60%. If you adjust speed frequently, choose 40%.
</Note>

### Haptic Feedback

Provides a subtle vibration when a gesture starts:

- Confirms that your swipe was recognized
- Helps distinguish between gestures and accidental touches
- Optional (can be disabled for silent operation)

To enable:

1. Go to **Settings** > **Player** > **Gestures**
2. Toggle **Haptic Feedback** on

## Gesture Conflict Prevention

The gesture system intelligently prevents conflicts:

### Edge Swipes

Swipes starting near screen edges are ignored to avoid conflicts with:
- Android system navigation gestures  
- Back gestures
- Status bar pull-down

**Edge threshold**: 30dp from screen edges (or system gesture insets)

### Controls Visible

When player controls are visible:
- Vertical swipes are disabled (prevents accidental volume/brightness changes)
- You can still interact with control buttons
- Swipe down from the top to minimize the player

### Progress Bar

When scrubbing the progress bar:
- All other gestures are disabled  
- Gesture indicators are hidden
- Touch focus remains on the scrubber

### Double-Tap

If double-tap is enabled (for cycling chat modes):
- Single taps show/hide controls
- Double taps cycle chat modes  
- Gestures remain available when controls are hidden

## Visual Feedback

All gestures display real-time visual feedback:

### Indicator Components

- **Icon**: Identifies the gesture type (volume, brightness, seek, speed)
- **Text**: Shows current value or position  
- **Progress bar**: Visualizes the level/position
- **Auto-hide**: Disappears 800ms after gesture ends

### Indicator Position

Feedback appears centered on screen, overlaying the video without obscuring important content.

## Troubleshooting

<Accordion title="Gestures aren't working">
  Check that:
  - You're in **landscape mode**
  - Player is **maximized** (not minimized)
  - Player **controls are hidden** (tap once to hide)
  - Gestures are **enabled** in settings
  - Your swipe **doesn't start near screen edges**
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Volume/brightness changes too much">
  Lower the sensitivity setting to **Low (0.5x)** for more precise control.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can't seek or change speed">
  These gestures only work for VODs and clips, not live streams. Verify you're watching recorded content.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Gestures conflict with controls">
  This is intentional. Gestures are disabled when controls are visible. Tap the video to hide controls, then use gestures.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Haptic feedback isn't working">
  - Ensure haptic feedback is enabled in ThystTV settings
  - Check that your device supports haptics
  - Verify that vibration isn't disabled in system settings
</Accordion>

## Advanced Tips

### Multi-Touch

The gesture system supports multi-touch:
- Only the first touch pointer is tracked
- Additional touches are ignored
- Release all fingers to end the gesture

### Gesture Locking

Once a gesture starts (e.g., volume), it "locks" to that mode:
- Prevents accidental mode switching mid-swipe  
- Lock releases when you lift your finger
- Allows continuous adjustment in one direction

### Sensitivity Tuning

For the best experience:
- **Tablets**: Use Low sensitivity (larger screen = larger swipes)  
- **Phones**: Use Medium or High sensitivity
- **Personal preference**: Experiment to find what feels natural
